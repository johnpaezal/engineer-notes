# Behavioral Interview
*Soft-skills round prep*

---

## Why It Matters
*Half the hiring decision*

**Weight** – ~50% of the final decision  
**Filters for** – Collaboration, ownership, communication, judgment  
**Premise** – Past behavior predicts future behavior  
**Goal** – Show you are someone people want to work with under pressure

Technical skill gets you in the room; behavioral answers decide if you stay.

---

## STAR Method
*Structure for every answer*

**Situation** – Context, briefly (1–2 lines)  
**Task** – Your specific responsibility or goal  
**Action** – What *you* did (use "I", not "we")  
**Result** – Outcome + what you learned, quantified if possible

Spend most time on **Action** and **Result**. Keep Situation short.

### Worked Example
*"Tell me about a time you improved something"*

> **S** – Our deploy pipeline took 40 min and broke often.  
> **T** – I was asked to make releases reliable before a launch.  
> **A** – I split the monolithic job into parallel stages, added caching, and wrote smoke tests that gated deploys.  
> **R** – Deploy time dropped to 12 min, failed releases went from ~1/week to near zero. I learned that small gating tests prevent most prod incidents.

## Common Questions
*Framework, not scripts*

### Conflict With a Teammate
*Probing: maturity, empathy*

Interviewer wants: do you attack the problem or the person.  
Approach: state the disagreement neutrally → show you sought their view → describe how you reached resolution → end on preserved relationship. Never make Alex the villain.

### Biggest Failure / A Mistake
*Probing: ownership, growth*

Interviewer wants: accountability + learning, not a fake weakness.  
Approach: pick a real mistake you owned → what you did to contain it → the concrete change you made so it never repeats. The learning is the point of the answer.

### Disagreed With a Decision
*Probing: disagree and commit*

Interviewer wants: you can push back respectfully, then align.  
Approach: how you raised concerns with data → who made the call → how you committed fully once decided, even if you disagreed. Show outcome honestly.

### Most Challenging Project
*Probing: scope, resilience*

Interviewer wants: real difficulty, your specific role.  
Approach: why it was hard (ambiguity / scale / deadline) → your concrete contribution → trade-offs you made → measurable result.

### Why AWS / Company / Role
*Probing: motivation, fit*

Interviewer wants: specific, researched reasons — not generic praise.  
Approach: tie the company's product/tech to your skills and where you want to grow. One genuine concrete reason beats five clichés.

### Tell Me About Yourself
*90-second present → past → future*

| Part | Content |
|---|---|
| Present | Who you are now + current focus |
| Past | 1–2 relevant experiences that built your skills |
| Future | Why this role is the logical next step |

Keep it ~90 seconds. It is a pitch, not a biography.

### Took Ownership / Beyond Scope
*Probing: initiative*

Interviewer wants: you act without being told.  
Approach: a problem nobody owned → you chose to take it → action + result. Show judgment, not just effort.

### Tight Deadlines / Competing Priorities
*Probing: prioritization*

Interviewer wants: a system, not heroics.  
Approach: how you assessed impact/urgency → what you negotiated or cut → how you communicated trade-offs to stakeholders → outcome.

## Red Flags to Avoid
*Instant negative signals*

**Blaming others** – "The team failed me" — never your fault  
**No learning** – Failure story with no change made after  
**Vague answers** – No specifics, all "we" and no "I"  
**Badmouthing** – Trashing past employers or managers  
**Rambling** – No structure, no point landed  
**Fake weakness** – "I work too hard" as a flaw

---

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer
*Signals seriousness and fit*

- What does success look like for this role in 6 months?
- How is the team structured and how do you collaborate?
- How does on-call / incident response work?
- How is technical debt tracked and prioritized?
- What growth or mentorship paths exist here?
- What is the biggest challenge the team faces right now?

Asking nothing is itself a red flag.

---

## Story Bank Approach
*Reshape few stories to many questions*

Prepare **5–6 strong stories** in STAR form. Each can answer multiple questions from different angles.

| Story type | Covers |
|---|---|
| A hard project | Challenge, ownership, deadlines |
| A conflict | Teamwork, communication, disagreement |
| A failure | Mistake, growth, accountability |
| A win you drove | Initiative, impact, leadership |
| A learning moment | Adaptability, feedback, growth |

### Prep Checklist
*Before the interview*

- [ ] 5–6 stories written in STAR, each quantified
- [ ] Each story maps to 2+ common questions
- [ ] "Tell me about yourself" rehearsed (~90s)
- [ ] 3 concrete reasons for *why this company/role*
- [ ] 4–5 questions ready to ask the interviewer
- [ ] Practiced out loud, said "I" not "we"
